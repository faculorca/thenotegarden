```
[Hook: Setting the Scene - 3 lines max, including blank lines]

"I was [describe mundane or routine setting, e.g., in a cab, in a café].

I was going to [unexpected or curiosity-provoking action]"  

[Introducing the Stranger]

"My [driver, barista, or another everyday character] was [describe their appearance or attitude briefly]."

  
[_Introduce the stranger with a simple description that makes them seem unremarkable at first._]

[Engaging in Small Talk]

"[Stranger] asked, '[small, innocent question, e.g., “What do you do?”]' which I answered without much thought."  

[_The conversation starts with small talk, allowing you to reveal something about yourself, usually in a casual or guarded way._]

[Discovering the Stranger’s Hidden Depths]

"Then [stranger] mentioned they [share surprising background, like owning a business or having a unique life story]. Now I was intrigued."

  
[_Reveal the stranger’s backstory, showing them to be much more complex than they initially appeared._]

[Revelation of Life Philosophy]

"They shared that [describe their philosophy or life lesson, often tied to a simple, repetitive activity like fishing]."

  
[_The stranger imparts a piece of wisdom or life philosophy based on their own experiences._]

[Analogy or Metaphor to Protagonist's Life]

"They said, '[describe their analogy or metaphor, e.g., comparing fishing to pursuing a passion],' which made me think about my own life."

  
[_The stranger’s story or analogy makes you question your current choices, especially something they’re neglecting or craving to pursue._]

[Protagonist’s Realization]

"Suddenly, I realized that [describe your internal struggle or what you've been avoiding]."

  
[_You connect the stranger’s story to your own life, sparking a revelation or wake-up call._]

[The Wake-Up Call]

"[Stranger] looked at me directly and said, '[direct piece of advice or encouragement, e.g., “Then write! And don’t let anyone stop you.”]'"

  
[_The stranger delivers a powerful, straightforward piece of advice that resonates deeply with you._]

[A Moment of Reflection]

"As I watched them [describe the stranger’s simple action, like driving away], I felt [describe the sense of clarity or renewed purpose]."

  
[_You reflect on the conversation, feeling a shift in perspective or commitment._]

[The Decision to Change]

"After that, I knew I had to [describe the first step toward their true calling, e.g., write every day]."

  
[_You decide to take action, influenced by the stranger’s advice, often involving a return to something you love but have neglected._]
```
